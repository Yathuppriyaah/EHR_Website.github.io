EHR stands for Electronic Health Record. It is a digital version of a patient's medical history that contains information such as their medical conditions, diagnoses, medications, allergies, immunization records, lab results, and other relevant clinical information. EHRs are used by healthcare providers to manage and coordinate a patient's care.

These records (EHRs) are increasingly being used to store and manage patient information, making healthcare more efficient and accessible. However, like any system that deals with sensitive personal information, EHRs must be secured to protect the patients' privacy and confidentiality.However, the use of EHRs also raises security concerns, as sensitive medical information can be vulnerable to unauthorized access or disclosure.With the widespread use of EHR, building a secure EHR sharing environment has attracted a lot of attention in both the healthcare industry and academic community in Srilanka.One of the biggest security issues in EHRs is data breaches in sensitive medical information.


Mission

The mission of a secure EHR (Electronic Health Record) system is to ensure that patient information is accessible, accurate, and secure. This includes protecting sensitive data from unauthorized access, ensuring that the data is complete and up-to-date, and providing authorized users with quick and efficient access to the information they need. .

vission

The vision for a secure EHR system is to create a centralized repository of patient information that can be accessed by authorized healthcare professionals anytime and anywhere. This will enable doctors and other healthcare providers to make informed decisions quickly and efficiently, leading to better patient outcomes.

End goals

The end goal of a secure EHR system is to improve the quality of healthcare and reduce medical errors. By providing healthcare providers with access to accurate and complete patient information, they can make better decisions, avoid duplicating tests and procedures, and prevent potentially harmful drug interactions.

Moment of find this idea

The implementation of Electronic Health Record systems in Sri Lanka has been attempted in various parts of the country, but it has yet to be implemented on a nationwide scale. Based on the analysis and literature review, it is widely recognized that EHR systems are at a high risk of data breaches due to the sensitive nature of the information they contain and the increasing sophistication of cybercriminals. According to a study by the Ponemon Institute, the healthcare industry experiences the highest number of data breaches of any industry, with over 60% of breaches being caused by hacking or IT incidents. .
